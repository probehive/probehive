# Community Standards

ProbeHive is committed to a professional, welcoming, and constructive project community.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate respectfully and assume good intent while evaluating technical claims on evidence.
- Give and receive specific, actionable feedback.
- Respect different backgrounds, experience levels, identities, and viewpoints.
- Keep discussions relevant to the project and its users.
- Protect private information and follow responsible disclosure practices.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, threats, or sustained personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberate disruption, spam, impersonation, or knowingly misleading technical claims.
- Retaliation against a person who reports a concern in good faith.

## Scope and Enforcement

These standards apply in repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, project communication channels, and public representation of the project.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner or maintainers through GitHub. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive personal information. Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue warnings, restrict participation, or ban participants when necessary to protect the community.

Enforcement decisions should consider context, severity, repeated behavior, impact, and the safety of affected people.
